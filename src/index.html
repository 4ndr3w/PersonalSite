FTC Team 4977

Alongside my work on FRC, I started my own FTC team with some friends from school. Through some of the crazy stuff we built for the FTC challenges, I learned a lot of the control theory I know now. All of our robots have their source on GitHub, check it out!

  • 2014 Robot - By far our simplest robot, while also being our most successful. This robot won multiple events from the #1 seed including the state championship. It went undefeated in qualifying matches at the PA State Championship and only dropped one match in the BO3 elimination rounds!
  • 2013 Robot - During the 2012 offseason, we developed a FTC legal swerve drive. The 2013 robot featured a competition-ready implementation of this drivetrain. Our control software supported robot-centric and field centric driving, as well as a shortest rotation algorithm for swerve pod angles. This robot was a finalist at the PA state championship!
  • 2012 Robot - This robot featured a large double jointed arm (relative to the size robot) that required me to learn about PID to control - controlling all components of the arm required three PID loops, with another two on the drivetrain during autonomous.
